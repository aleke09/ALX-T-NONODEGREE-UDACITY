Part II - (Investigating Trip features)¶

by (Aleke Ezekiel Victor)¶

Investigation Overview¶

I intended to look at the trip data characteristics that could be used to forecast their duration in this inquiry. Age, start station, finish station, user type, and gender were given the most attention.

Dataset Overview¶

The data consists of duration_sec, age and user_type with 183,412. The attributes included the five, as well as additional measurements such as start station name, start station latitude, and start station longitude.

Distribution of Trip Durations¶

Number of Trips values initially rise from 8000 to 12500 at the peak, which lasts for around 600 seconds, before beginning to decline at 2000 values.

Distribution of User Age¶

In the case of age, the distribution is more concentrated between 20 to 40 years old.

Trip Duration by Age¶

Frequent duration is registered by younger members between 20 and 45.

Trip Duration by Age and Gender¶

The main thing I want to explore in this part of the analysis is how the three categorical measures of gender into the relationship between trip duration and age.

Trip Duration by Age and User Type¶

The main thing I want to explore in this part of the analysis is how the three categorical measures of user type into the relationship between trip duration and age.

For the age, duration, and user type, both Customer and Subscriber are showing similar trends for age and trip duration, but for subscribers the trip duration is higher for older age.
